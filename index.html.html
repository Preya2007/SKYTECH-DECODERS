🔧 Project Introduction

📋 Problem Statement

Develop a dual image super-resolution system for high-resolution optical satellite imagery that can enhance image quality and resolution while implementing blind evaluation metrics to assess enhancement quality without requiring ground truth reference images.

🌍 Importance in Satellite Imagery

Disaster Management: Enhanced imagery for better damage assessment
Urban Planning: Detailed city development monitoring
Agriculture: Precise crop monitoring and yield prediction
Environmental: Forest cover and climate change analysis

🔄 What is Dual Image Super-Resolution?

A technique that uses two low-resolution satellite images of the same scene (potentially from different angles or times) to generate a single high-resolution output. This approach leverages complementary information from both images to achieve better enhancement than single-image methods.

👁️ What is Blind Evaluation?

Quality assessment of enhanced images without access to ground truth high-resolution references. Uses perceptual metrics, statistical analysis, and subjective evaluation to determine enhancement quality in real-world scenarios where perfect references don't exist.
